Firefox has a lot of great features that make the internet better.
  • Adblock Plus
    Blocks ads on webpages.
  • Coral IE Tab
    Enables you to use the embedded IE engine within tabs of Mozilla Firefox.
  • Download Statusbar
    View and manage downloads from a tidy statusbar.
  • FireGestures
    Control movement within firefox with gestures from your mouse.
  • Ubiquity
    Allows for the use of dynamic commands in Firefox.
  • Xmarks
    Keeps your bookmarks and (optionally) passwords backed up and synchronized.
Additional Extensions that may be useful for you.
  • BugMeNot
    Bypass compulsory web registration with the context menu.
  • CookieSafe
    Allows you to easily control cookie permissions.
  • Customize Google
    Enhances Google search results by removing unwanted information (like ads and spam).
  • DownThemAll!
    The first and only download manager/accelerator built inside Firefox.
  • eSnipe
    eSnipe's service that will allow you to place a bid snipe directly from the eBay item page.
Unsung Hero
My favorite extension for Firefox is Ubiquity and believe it or not, it's not one of the most popular extensions around.  Ubiquity gives you the ability to select something on a webpage then issue a command to highlight the text, map the address, email the page to someone, translate the text to another language, or search popular video sites for videos about your search term.  Ubiquity is absolutely one of the greatest extensions created for Firefox.
